# Design Process — Iterations & Decisions

## What I Started With

- Initial idea: a simple letter-based logotype using my initials
- Felt too generic — no symbolic meaning

## What Changed

### Iteration 1 — Initials only
Dropped. Could have been anyone's mark.

### Iteration 2 — Abstract shape exploration
Had a confilct with choosing between my cultural and technical identity.  

### Iteration 3 — Split concept
The idea of a vertical seam emerged from thinking about the actual tension in my identity —
technical thinking vs. cultural thinking. Neither is decorative. Both are real.

### Iteration 4 — Neural network + Basotho hat
Left half became a neural/brain network diagram (logic, DSA, coding, networking).
Right half became a witch hat silhouette (culture, intuition, the non-technical self).
The glowing seam between them is the mark itself.

## What I Removed

- Colour (originally tried purple on the left side)
- Text labels inside the mark
- A third element (was going to add initials below the seam)
- Decorative border around the whole image

## What Stayed

- The binary split
- The white-on-black palette
- The glow effect on the seam — it felt earned, not decorative
- The neural network nodes: each one is a connection, not just decoration

## The Constraint That Helped Most

*"It must work in black and white."*
This forced every element to carry weight through shape and contrast, not colour.

*"It must be simple enough to redraw by hand"*
This forced me to leave my initial drawing and go for simple node connecting each other for the brain.
